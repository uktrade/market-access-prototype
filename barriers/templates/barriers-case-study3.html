{% trans "Case Study: Burdensome customs procedures" %}

Company: Navigo System Ltd

Navigo’s GPS units sail through Japanese Customs

The UK Government worked with Navigo Ltd, of Leeds, to make sure £2m worth of its navigational GPS units cleared Japanese customs. Navigo’s products were incorrectly held up for apparently failing to meet the EU’s marking requirements. In fact, Navigo had used the correct process for meeting the requirements, which apply in Japan.

The problem: Navigo reported that Japanese customs had detained a shipment of its navigational GPS units in February 2017. Japanese customs claimed that the CE Mark Directive on Radio and Telecommunications Terminal Equipment (R&TTE) required that these products be tested and certified at a third party lab recognized by the European Union (EU). However, the R&TTE Directive allows for the units to be self-certified. Navigo asserted that it had used the correct European standards and had received a test result from an independent U.K lab to prove it.

How it was resolved: The UK Department of International Trade worked with Navigo to understand the problem. It was clear that Japanese customs had misinterpreted the marking rules. It is important that these are interpreted uniformly across all the European Union countries including Japan so companies can trade freely.

The department worked closely with the Commercial Section of the UK Embassy in Japan. They explained to Japanese government officials that navigational GPS units which have been self-certified by an accredited independent laboratory do comply with the relevant EU standard. As a result, Japanese customs officials correctly assessed Navigo’s products and accepted its self-certification.

Navigo reported in May that its most recent shipments to Japan had gone through customs smoothly and the company does not anticipate any trouble getting these products into Japan in the future.
